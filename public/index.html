Save Marsden Point Oil Refinery

Marsden Point Refinery is a critical piece of infrastructure which until recently provided most of NZ’s transport fuel, roading bitumen as well as CO2 for food processing and sulphur for fertlizer1. And is slated for destruction by corrupt Fuel Retailing Cartel enabled by naive and incompetent Government Green Ideologues, as the country moves to an import only model which exposes NZ to supply, price and quality risks, exports all the GDP associated with the refinery and removes options for energy development.

In 1988 Refining NZ got a $2 billon refinery paid for by the taxpayer. The main shareholders, the fuel retailing cartel sucked profits out of the refinery and into their downstream businesses over many years using their controversial (70/30) fee structure. Even given the existence of much more efficient mega-refineries principally in Asia, there is obviously increased supply chain risk in having these as the only source of refined products. The government appears to have been influenced by emissions reduction goals not to offer to help the refinery through the diffcult times of the Covid pandemic. The almost comic incompetence of the decision to close the refinery was immediately showcased with the refining business surging to historic profitability as supply chain and geopolitical risks skyrocket.

The Fuel Retailing Cartel have asset stripped the refinery they were gifted by the NZ public in a way which would shame a Yeltsin-era Russian oligarch. – River Of Filth Tyrrany Response Team

The refinery is currently in the state of a regular shutdown but can still be saved is we act quickly as the government has done for Air NZ, Tiwai Point Ihumatao. In fact a cabinet paper Megan Woods propesed a loan to save the refinery which was rejected by cabinet. A motive for this economic sabotage became clear when James Shaw announced his emissions budgets noting in a cabinet paper the accounting trick of exporting our refinery emissions (along with some GDP) while probably actually increasing global emissions.
